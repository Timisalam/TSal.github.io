The 6"5 shooting guard was born and raised in Greenfield Wisconsin. He started playing basketball at an early age and became a regular member of his school team while studying in the Whitnall High School in Wisconsin. During his senior year, he had an average of 32.9 points per game including 7.4 rebounds and 3.6 assists. He also proved his worth during that time while shooting better than 50% from the floor and 43.5% from behind the triple-scoring line.

Tyler originally committed to the university of Wisconsin but after Kentucky coach, John Calipari went to watch one of his games personally he was offered a full scholarship which he graciously accepted.In college, Herro got an average of 14.0 points, 4.5 rebounds, 2.5 assists, and became the only player to start in all 37 games played by the Kentucky Wildcats.He hit a collegiate career-high 29 points to lead the Wildcats to a HUGE comeback win over the Arkansas Razorbacks.This was his coming out party, he soon after declared for the nba draft.During his college career, his fellow students named him ‘Boy Wonder’ due to his impeccable skills and unmatched shooting skills.

Tyler was projected to go mid first round before being selected 13th overall by the Miami Heat, comparisons to Bradley Beal and Devin booker, others called him Klay Thompson with handles.His fellow basketball rookies named him as the best shooter of that draft class. He went on to average 13.5 points, 4.4 rebounds and 2.2 assists and managed the most three-pointers made by a rookie in N.B.A. Playoffs ever, accomplishing 45 beyond-the-arc shots while outshining Matt Maloney’s 43.3 PM record during the 1997 NBA Playoffs whilst helping miami get to the Nba Finals
